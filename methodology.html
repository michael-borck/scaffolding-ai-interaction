Research Methodology

Study Design and Procedures

Research Design

Randomised controlled experiment. Individual-level randomisation to nudge vs control condition within structured lab sessions across three or more university units spanning Information Systems, Supply Chain Management, and Marketing.

All sessions use a frontier-class AI model (Claude or GPT-4 class) accessed via API. This is a deliberate design choice: to isolate the effect of the nudge, the underlying model must be capable enough that variation in output quality reflects student interaction rather than model limitations.

Participants and Recruitment

Population: Curtin students enrolled in participating units (ISYS6018 Information Security Audit and Control, MGMT6076 Supply Chain Digitalisation and Innovation, MKTG1000 Discovering Marketing). Participation is voluntary and does not affect grades.

Target sample: Combined enrolment across three units is approximately 300 students (ISYS6018 ~50, MGMT6076 ~30, MKTG1000 ~220). Estimated 100-200 voluntary participants. A power analysis will be finalised once participation rates are confirmed.

Recruitment: Via PI and co-investigators with plain-language invitations on LMS and email. Session timing varied to accommodate students with work or caring commitments. Purposive encouragement of participation from low SES and first-in-family students.

Inclusion: Enrolled students in participating units; aged 18+; able to attend scheduled lab sessions.

Exclusion: Students under 18; students who do not consent; students without capacity to consent.

The Intervention

Session Format

Each session follows a consistent format (45-60 minutes):

  1. Student logs in with student number (assigned a random session code)
  2. Student is randomly assigned to nudge or control condition
  3. Student completes a discipline-relevant task using the AI chat interface
  4. Conversations are logged automatically throughout
  5. 10-minute exit survey via Qualtrics on completion

The AI interface is provided and configured by the research team. Students use lab computers; no personal account or device is required.

The Conversational Nudge

In the nudge condition, a short prompt is appended to every AI response. To reduce habituation, the prompt is randomly selected each turn from a pool of 10 variants. All variants follow the same design principles:

  • Invite pushback, not confirmation
  • Domain-agnostic (work across all disciplines)
  • No prior AI experience assumed
  • Make the conversational path easier, not mandatory

Example variants:

“Does that match what you expected? If something seems off, tell me what seems wrong.”

“What’s the weakest part of that response?”

“Does that account for the details in your task, or is it a generic answer?”

The nudge is appended server-side after the AI response. The AI model never sees the nudge text - it is not included in the conversation history sent to the API. This ensures the AI’s behaviour is identical across conditions.

See Nudge Variants for the full pool.

System Prompt

A system prompt instructs the AI model to answer directly without follow-up suggestions, engagement hooks, or offers to elaborate. This prevents the model’s default behaviour from contaminating the control condition (frontier models are trained to keep users engaged, which would create pseudo-nudges in both conditions).

Task Briefs

Each discipline has a primary conversational task requiring iterative dialogue to develop a recommendation or analysis:

  • Information Systems - AI demand forecasting recommendation
  • Supply Chain Management - supply chain resilience briefing
  • Marketing - retail channel strategy assessment

Retrieval-framed task variants are designed for a planned follow-on study.

Measures

Automated Measures

Computed directly from transcript logs via script. No human coding required.

Measure Definition
turns_total Total student turns
turns_coded Turns coded on behavioural dimensions (Turn 2+)
words_mean_student Average words per student turn
question_marks_proportion Proportion of turns with a question mark
duration_minutes Session duration
response_latency_mean Mean time between AI response and student reply

Human-Coded Behavioural Dimensions

The RA codes 100% of transcripts from Turn 2 onward (Turn 1 is excluded as it cannot be a follow-up, challenge, or extension). Three binary dimensions per turn:

Dimension Code Definition
Follow-Up (FU) 0/1 Did the student ask a question that continues the conversation?
Challenge (CH) 0/1 Did the student push back on, doubt, or correct the AI?
Extension (EX) 0/1 Did the student introduce new information or a new angle?

Delegation is derived in analysis (FU=0, CH=0, EX=0), not coded separately. This eliminates the possibility of contradictory data.

Turn-Level Scoring (Dual)

Score Formula Range Rationale
Unweighted FU + CH + EX 0-3 Treats all dimensions equally
Challenge-weighted FU + 2xCH + EX 0-4 Emphasises Challenge, the dimension most directly targeted by the nudge

Both scores are reported. If they converge, the finding is robust to scoring assumptions. If they diverge, it reveals whether the nudge primarily drives challenge behaviour or broader engagement.

Task Completion Score

Scored by co-investigators on their own unit’s transcripts. Scale 1-5 (minimal output to comprehensive analysis). Not comparable across units - discipline-specific rubric anchors.

Exit Survey

Administered via Qualtrics. Captures:

  • Confidence calibration (Q2 vs task score - within-unit only)
  • Self-reported vs observed interaction style (Q5/Q6 vs coded transcript)
  • Condition awareness (Q8a - did nudge students notice the nudge?)
  • Prior AI experience (Q9/Q10 - baseline covariate)

See Exit Survey for the full instrument.

Data Collection and De-identification

  1. Student enters student number at session login. System assigns a random session code and records a temporary linkage.
  2. All research data (transcripts, task outputs, survey) tagged with session code only.
  3. After data collection closes, the linkage table is used to match equity flags (low SES, first-in-family) from Curtin SIMS.
  4. A de-identification script strips student numbers. The linkage table is permanently destroyed. Destruction is logged and auditable.
  5. The research team analyses a fully de-identified dataset.

Analysis Plan

Primary Analysis

Linear mixed-effects model accounting for the nested structure (students within units):

outcome ~ condition + (1 | unit)
  • outcome: session-level measure (e.g., mean_turn_score_raw, proportion_challenge)
  • condition: fixed effect (Nudge vs Control)
  • (1 | unit): random intercept for unit

Why mixed-effects: Students are not independent across units. Each unit has a different task, discipline, and facilitator. Simple ANOVA ignores this nesting and underestimates standard errors.

Covariate: Prior AI experience (Q10) included as a fixed effect if groups differ at baseline.

Equity-Disaggregated Analysis

outcome ~ condition * equity_flag + (1 | unit)

A significant interaction indicates the nudge effect differs by equity cohort (differential benefit). A non-significant interaction with a significant main effect indicates uniform benefit. Both findings are reported.

Confidence Calibration

Conducted within unit only (task scores not comparable across disciplines):

  • Spearman correlation between self-reported confidence and task score
  • Comparison of the confidence-performance gap between conditions
  • Identification of the “overconfident delegator” pattern

Inter-Rater Reliability

  • RA codes 100% of transcripts on FU, CH, EX
  • Each co-investigator independently codes a 20-30% stratified sample from their unit
  • Cohen’s kappa calculated per unit
  • Kappa < 0.70 triggers scheme revision before full analysis

See Coding Scheme for the complete specification.

Reporting

All analyses reported with:

  • Effect sizes (Cohen’s d; marginal R-squared for mixed models)
  • 95% confidence intervals
  • Both unweighted and weighted scoring results
  • Equity-disaggregated results regardless of significance

Implementation: R with lme4, or Python with statsmodels.

Ethical Considerations

HREC application submitted under Curtin’s low-risk review pathway. See the Ethics Package for the full application and supporting documents.

Key protections:

  • Student numbers collected at login for equity linkage only; destroyed after de-identification
  • All research data uses session codes (no names, no student IDs)
  • Participation voluntary, does not affect grades
  • No-penalty alternative activity available
  • Withdrawal possible until de-identification
  • Nudge transparently described in PIS (no deception)
